\title{Regular Sets and Expressions}
\author{Alexander Krauss and Tobias Nipkow}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This is a library of constructions on regular expressions and languages.  It
provides the operations of concatenation, Kleene star and derivative on
languages. Regular expressions and their meaning are defined.  An executable
equivalence checker for regular expressions is verified; it does not need
automata but works directly on regular expressions. \emph{By mapping regular
expressions to binary relations, an automatic and complete proof method for
(in)equalities of binary relations over union, concatenation and (reflexive)
transitive closure is obtained.}
\end{abstract}
